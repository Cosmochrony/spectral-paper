\subsection{Spectral proximity}
  \label{subsec:spectral-proximity}

  Given the Laplacian spectrum $\{\lambda_n, \phi_n\}$, spectral kernels define
  relational proximity between nodes.
  A canonical example is the heat kernel
  \begin{equation}
    K(i,j;\alpha)
    \;=\;
    \sum_{n} e^{-\alpha \lambda_n}\,\phi_n(i)\,\phi_n(j),
  \end{equation}
  where $\alpha$ sets the spectral scale.

  This kernel measures connectivity between elements $i$ and $j$
  through the spectrum of $\Delta$, without reference to metric distance.
  Such constructions are standard in clustering and manifold learning~\cite{VonLuxburg2007}.
  They are invariant under node relabeling and require no embedding structure.
