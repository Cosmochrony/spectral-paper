\subsection{Static Spherically Symmetric Geometry}
  \label{subsec:schwarzschild-recovery}

  Consider a static, approximately spherically symmetric relational
  configuration admitting a smooth continuum limit.
  The analysis is purely kinematic and constrained by symmetry and
  weak-field consistency.

  The effective metric takes the standard form
  \begin{equation}
    ds^2 = -A(r)\,c^2 dt^2 + B(r)\,dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2 ,
  \end{equation}
  where $A(r)$ and $B(r)$ encode the operational relation between
  temporal and spatial intervals.

  In the weak-field regime,
  \begin{equation}
    A(r) \simeq 1 + 2\frac{\Phi(r)}{c^2},
    \qquad
    B(r) \simeq \left(1 + 2\frac{\Phi(r)}{c^2}\right)^{-1},
  \end{equation}
  with $\Phi(r)$ characterizing deviations from flat geometry.
  No dynamical origin for $\Phi$ is assumed.

  Imposing asymptotic flatness and spherical symmetry
  fixes the exterior solution at leading order,
  \begin{equation}
    A(r) = 1 - \frac{r_s}{r},
    \qquad
    B(r) = \left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r}\right)^{-1},
  \end{equation}
  where $r_s$ is an integration constant.
  This coincides with the Schwarzschild geometry at leading order.

  Standard weak-field effects—gravitational redshift,
  light deflection, and time dilation—follow directly
  from the metric structure as kinematic consequences.
