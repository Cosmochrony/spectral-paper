\subsection{Combinatorial vs.\ weighted distance}
  \label{subsec:combinatorial-vs.-weighted-distance}

  We distinguish two path-based distances defined on the relational graph.

  \begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Combinatorial distance} (pre-geometric):
    \[
      d_{ij}^{C} = \min_{\gamma_{ij}} \sum_{(u,v)\in\gamma_{ij}} 1,
    \]
    where $\gamma_{ij}$ ranges over paths connecting $i$ and $j$.
    This hop-count distance depends only on adjacency and is independent
    of the field $\chi$.

    \item \textbf{Weighted distance} (effective):
    \[
      d_{ij}^{W} = \min_{\gamma_{ij}} \sum_{(u,v)\in\gamma_{ij}} w_{uv},
    \]
    where $w_{uv} > 0$ are link weights encoding relational stiffness.
    This distance underlies the emergent geometric constructions.
  \end{enumerate}

  The separation ensures that the coarse-grained background
  $\bar{\chi}$ is defined using $d_{ij}^{C}$ alone, while
  the effective geometry is encoded by $d_{ij}^{W}$ through
  weights depending only on $\bar{\chi}$.
  The combinatorial distance plays a purely auxiliary role.
