\subsection{Combinatorial vs.\ weighted distance}
  \label{subsec:combinatorial-vs.-weighted-distance}

  We distinguish two path-based distances defined on the relational graph.

  \begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Combinatorial distance} (pre-geometric):
    \[
      d_{ij}^{C} = \min_{\gamma_{ij}} \sum_{(u,v)\in\gamma_{ij}} 1,
    \]
    where $\gamma_{ij}$ ranges over paths connecting $i$ and $j$.
    This hop-count distance depends only on adjacency.

    \item \textbf{Weighted distance} (effective):
    \[
      d_{ij}^{W} = \min_{\gamma_{ij}} \sum_{(u,v)\in\gamma_{ij}} w_{uv},
    \]
    where $w_{uv} > 0$ are link weights encoding relational stiffness.
    This distance underlies the emergent geometric constructions.
  \end{enumerate}

  To avoid circularity, the weights $w_{uv}$ are not defined from $d^W$ itself
  but from local neighbourhood aggregates $\bar{\chi}_i$ computed using $d^C$ alone.
  Let $N_i = \{ j \mid d_{ij}^C \le \ell_0 \}$ be the combinatorial neighbourhood
  of radius $\ell_0$ around node~$i$.
  The local aggregate at node~$i$ is
  \begin{equation}
    \bar{\chi}_i \;=\; \frac{1}{|N_i|} \sum_{j \in N_i} \chi_j,
    \label{eq:chi-bar}
  \end{equation}
  where $\chi_j$ denotes the local relational observable at node~$j$.
  The values $\chi_j$ are graph-level descriptors with no ontological status
  as a fundamental field; they serve solely as inputs to the weight calibration
  in Section~\ref{subsec:weight-functional-and-positivity}.
  Because $N_i$ depends only on $d^C$, the aggregate $\bar{\chi}$ is
  \textbf{independent of any weighted metric}.

  The scale $\ell_0$ is an auxiliary coarse-graining parameter; it does not
  correspond to a physical length scale prior to the emergence of geometry.
  The combinatorial distance plays a purely auxiliary role.
