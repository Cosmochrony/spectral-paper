\subsection{Outlook}
  \label{subsec:outlook}

  The present study isolates the emergence of metric geometry from
  purely relational spectral organization.
  Extensions toward non-geometric regimes, matter-like excitations,
  or quantum and statistical phenomena require additional structural
  assumptions and are left for future work.

  The results show that a broad class of gravitational behaviors
  can arise from spectral organization alone.
  Geometry thus appears not as a fundamental arena,
  but as a derived and regime-dependent descriptor
  of deeper relational structure.
